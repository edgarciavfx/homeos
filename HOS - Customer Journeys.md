# Introduction

HomeOS exists to help households transform intentions into consistent action.

The following customer journeys describe how users currently manage household responsibilities, where friction occurs, and how HomeOS creates value by improving planning, prioritization, and execution.

These journeys are organized around the highest-confidence household workflows identified during discovery.

---

# Journey 1 — Weekly Household Planning

## Objective

Create a realistic and coordinated plan for the upcoming week.

---

## Current State

At the start of each week, the household attempts to mentally coordinate:

- Work obligations
    
- Household responsibilities
    
- Groceries
    
- Meals
    
- Cleaning
    
- Personal commitments
    
- Financial priorities
    

Information exists across:

- Memory
    
- Conversations
    
- Calendars
    
- Notes
    
- Messaging applications
    

Planning often happens informally or not at all.

---

## Current Pain Points

- Important responsibilities are overlooked
    
- Priorities are unclear
    
- Household members make different assumptions
    
- Time requirements are underestimated
    
- The week becomes reactive rather than intentional
    

---

## Trigger

Sunday evening arrives.

The household wants to prepare for the upcoming week.

---

## HomeOS Experience

### Step 1 — Weekly Review

HomeOS presents:

- Upcoming calendar events
    
- Household responsibilities
    
- Outstanding tasks
    
- Household rhythm status
    
- Financial considerations
    

---

### Step 2 — Priority Selection

The household identifies:

- Top priorities
    
- Important obligations
    
- Desired outcomes
    

Examples:

- Grocery shopping
    
- Laundry completion
    
- Meal preparation
    
- Upcoming purchases
    

---

### Step 3 — Weekly Plan Generation

HomeOS recommends:

- Household priorities
    
- Responsibility assignments
    
- Suggested timing
    
- Quick wins
    
- Potential conflicts
    

---

### Step 4 — Approval

Users review recommendations and make adjustments.

The household remains in control.

---

## Outcome

The household begins the week with:

- Shared expectations
    
- Clear priorities
    
- Visible responsibilities
    
- Reduced uncertainty
    

---

## Success Metrics

- Weekly plans created consistently
    
- Increased planning confidence
    
- Reduced reactive decision-making
    

---

# Journey 2 — Meal & Grocery Planning

## Objective

Plan meals and grocery purchases before food decisions become urgent.

---

## Current State

Typical pattern:

Need Food  
↓  
No Meal Plan  
↓  
No Ingredients  
↓  
Takeout

Grocery shopping is often reactive.

Meal decisions are frequently made when household members are already hungry.

---

## Current Pain Points

- Uncertainty about what to cook
    
- Repetitive meals
    
- Frequent takeout
    
- Forgotten ingredients
    
- Food waste
    
- Inefficient grocery trips
    

---

## Trigger

The weekly planning session begins.

The household needs meals for the upcoming week.

---

## HomeOS Experience

### Step 1 — Meal Recommendation

HomeOS proposes meals based on:

- Household preferences
    
- Nutritional goals
    
- Familiar dishes
    
- Available preparation time
    
- Budget considerations
    

---

### Step 2 — Meal Approval

Users review recommendations.

They may:

- Accept meals
    
- Replace meals
    
- Rearrange meals
    

Final approval remains with the household.

---

### Step 3 — Grocery Planning

HomeOS generates:

- Required ingredients
    
- Suggested quantities
    
- Store-specific shopping lists
    

---

### Step 4 — Shopping Preparation

The household receives a clear plan before visiting stores.

Shopping becomes execution rather than decision-making.

---

## Outcome

The household gains:

- Planned meals
    
- Accurate grocery lists
    
- Reduced takeout dependence
    
- Reduced food waste
    

---

## Success Metrics

- Reduced takeout frequency
    
- Increased meal preparation
    
- Reduced grocery uncertainty
    
- Reduced food waste

# Journey 3 — Household Rhythm Management

## Objective

Maintain a consistently functional home through predictable routines.

---

## Current State

Household maintenance is managed reactively.

Common pattern:

Laundry Accumulates  
↓  
Cleaning Delayed  
↓  
Visible Clutter  
↓  
Stress Increases  
↓  
Large Cleanup Session

The problem is not awareness.

The problem is cadence.

---

## Current Pain Points

- Laundry becomes overwhelming
    
- Cleaning feels endless
    
- Small tasks accumulate
    
- Household condition fluctuates significantly
    

---

## Trigger

A new week begins.

The household wants to avoid backlog accumulation.

---

## HomeOS Experience

### Step 1 — Rhythm Review

HomeOS reviews:

- Daily activities
    
- Weekly activities
    
- Biweekly activities
    
- Maintenance obligations
    

---

### Step 2 — Schedule Recommendations

HomeOS suggests:

- Laundry windows
    
- Cleaning sessions
    
- Trash removal timing
    
- Maintenance activities
    

Recommendations consider available household time.

---

### Step 3 — Ownership Visibility

The household can clearly see:

- Who owns each responsibility
    
- Shared obligations
    
- Upcoming commitments
    

---

### Step 4 — Weekly Execution

Tasks are distributed throughout the week rather than accumulating.

---

## Outcome

The household experiences:

- More consistent cleanliness
    
- Fewer overwhelming catch-up sessions
    
- Better maintenance habits
    

---

## Success Metrics

- Reduced overdue chores
    
- Improved routine consistency
    
- Improved household condition

# End-to-End Household Journey

The complete HomeOS experience follows a recurring cycle.

## Sunday

### Plan

The household reviews:

- Priorities
    
- Meals
    
- Groceries
    
- Responsibilities
    
- Financial considerations
    

HomeOS generates a coordinated weekly plan.

---

## During the Week

### Execute

Users receive:

- Daily guidance
    
- Meal visibility
    
- Responsibility visibility
    
- Household rhythm support
    

The focus is execution rather than planning.

---

## End of Week

### Review

The household evaluates:

- Completed priorities
    
- Missed activities
    
- Grocery outcomes
    
- Household condition
    

---

## Reset

Insights feed into the next planning session.

The cycle repeats.

---

# Journey Design Principles

All HomeOS journeys must follow these principles:

## Guide, Don't Control

Recommendations support decisions.

Users remain responsible for final approval.

---

## Planning Over Reminders

Planning creates value.

Reminders exist to support execution.

---

## Reduce Decision Fatigue

Every recommendation should simplify choices.

---

## Avoid Guilt-Based Experiences

Missed plans should lead to adaptation, not punishment.

---

## Convert Intentions Into Action

Every journey should move users closer to consistent household execution.

---

# Summary

The customer journey of HomeOS is not about managing tasks, groceries, finances, or chores independently.

It is about helping households move through a continuous cycle:

Intentions  
↓  
Planning  
↓  
Prioritization  
↓  
Execution  
↓  
Review  
↓  
Improvement

HomeOS succeeds when households feel more proactive, more organized, less overwhelmed, and more confident that important responsibilities will actually get done.
